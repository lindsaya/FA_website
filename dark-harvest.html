About the Project

Based on Norman Partridge's Bram Stoker Award-winning novel, Dark Harvest is set in a small Midwestern town gripped by a deadly annual ritual. Every Halloween, teenagers armed with weapons must race across town and kill Sawtooth Jack — a monstrous entity born from the cornfields — before he reaches the local church. The one who does becomes a local legend.

Directed by David Slade for MGM, the film is a visceral, stylised creature horror that required extensive VFX to bring Sawtooth Jack and his terrifying world to life.

Our Work

Future Associate delivered 200+ VFX shots across the production, with Sawtooth Jack at the centre of the work. Rather than a full CG replacement, the team developed a hybrid approach: the actor's on-set performance was 3D scanned in full costume, then digitally sculpted to warp the humanoid form into the creature's skeletal proportions — preserving in-camera lighting and movement throughout.

A BlendShape setup in Nuke drove the warping, with selective CG patching where the warp alone wasn't enough. Some shots are fully CG; others are almost entirely what was captured in-camera. CG glowing head, fire effects, and close-up facial animations were composited using chrome balls, LiDAR scans, and clean plates to lock the lighting.
